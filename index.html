Race Rules:

The pre-race briefing (at 8:00AM on Saturday, Oct 10th in transition) is mandatory
*Athletes must move forward after the start signal
*Blocking or interfering with the forward progress of another competitor is not allowed.
*Helmet chin-strap MUST be buckled at all times when touching the bike
*Athletes overtaken (lapped) by the race leader on the bike course will be disqualified
*Unsportsmanlike conduct, including inappropriate language directed at fellow competitors, volunteers, or officials, may result in disqualification

*Trisuit or 2 Piece Trisuit allowed. Must be covering the torso area.
*No Aero Helmets Permitted/Must have individual helmet with chin-strap.
*Each participant must have their own bicycle. Bicycles must be triangular frame, with drop-style handlebars. Aerobars must not extend past the brake levers of the bicycle and must be capped and bridged together. Additionally, no bar-end gear shifters allowed.
*USAT will be checking all bicycles and equipment before allowing participants to enter transition in the morning. S/O to UCSD for inspiration <3

MIXED TEAM RELAY

Course Information For Draft Legal Individuals

A highly exciting and highly competitive sprint distance, draft-legal race with distances of 500 meters for the swim, 11.3 miles for the bike, and 3.1 miles for the run. The collegiate men and community men will race together in the same wave, and the collegiate women and community women will race together in the second wave.

Participants:

*Only 75 men in wave 1 (Men) and only 75 women in wave 2 (Women).
*Only 5 men/5 women from each collegiate team please. This restriction will be lifted on October 3, 2015 if there are remaining spots.
*35 spots reserved for community members in each draft legal wave.

Rules and Additional Draft Legal Information:

Please read through the "General Info" tab as well as the USAT Draft Legal Rules Summary here

SWIM COURSE

Swim course: ~500 meters, 1 lap
The swim is an open water start, with athletes treading water at the start line until the gun goes off. Racers will swim clockwise towards the buoys, before heading back towards the dock where they will be pulled out of the water. Wetsuits are recommended.

TRANSITION #1

Transition 1: 0.1 miles
Racers will then follow the road to transition, at a grassy field roughly half a lap away.

BIKE COURSE

Bike Course: 11.3 miles, total of 3 laps
The bike is a fast and flat course with great drafting capability. Racers will come out of transition, taking a right onto University Ave, eventually exiting the Berkeley Marina area when turning right onto W. Frontage Rd, which runs parellel to the 580 Highway and the San Francisco Bay Trail. Racers will stay on W. Frontage Rd for a total of 3 laps, each including 2 U-turns, before returning onto University Ave and heading into Transition 2.

TRANSITION #2

Transition 2:
The same location as transition 1, racers will dismount their bikes and start the run portion of the race from here.

RUN COURSE

Run Course: 3.1 miles
The run begins with a very quick and flat 1st mile. The 2nd mile is a mildly hilly run around Cesar Chavez Park with stellar views of the San Francisco Bay and the Golden Gate Bridge. Runners will return along the same route they started on for that exciting sprint finish and some tasty post-race snacks.

Course Information For Mixed Team Relay

One of the most exciting races to watch is the mixed team relay! The triathlon mixed team relay consists of teams of four, comprised of two men and two women. The women go 1st and 3rd, while the men go 2nd and 4th. Each athlete completes a super-sprint, draft-legal triathlon, before tagging their teammate. The race will be 250 meters for the swim meters for the swim, 4.5 miles for the bike, and 1.2 miles for the run.

Rules and Additional Draft Legal Information:

Please read through the "General Info" tab as well as the USAT Draft Legal Rules Summary here

SWIM COURSE

Swim course: 250 meters for the swim meters, 1 lap
The swim is an open water start, with athletes treading water at the start line until the gun goes off. Racers will swim clockwise towards the buoys, before heading back towards the dock where they will be pulled out of the water.

TRANSITION #1

Transition 1: 0.1 miles
Racers will then follow the road to transition, at a grassy field roughly half a lap away.

BIKE COURSE

Bike course: 4.5 miles, 1 lap
The bike course is a quick 4.5 mile out and back along the bay on Frontage road. It is a fast and flat road, with no climbs and one tight u-turn. Drafting is allowed during this portion of the race.

TRANSITION #2

Transition 2:
The same location as transition 1, racers will dismount their bikes and start the run portion of the race from here.

RUN COURSE

Run course: 1.2 miles, 1 lap
The run course is also a fast and flat out and back, where racers run just over half a mile on the San Francisco Bay trail before turning around. The run finishes at the dock, where racers will run to tag the next person in the relay. The last racer, however, will finish at the same finish as the draft legal race.
